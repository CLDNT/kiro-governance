Risk
• Risk of project managers making incorrect Git commits or using wrong branches (technical error) — raised as a usability/accuracy risk; impact: corrupted or misplaced artifacts and governance tracking; mitigation: avoid requiring PMs to use Git by providing the new app for marking gates and uploading evidence.
• Risk of artifacts being stored in multiple locations (SharePoint/Teams vs Git) leading to confusion or duplication — raised as a documentation/operational risk; impact: inability to locate definitive artifacts; mitigation: store links or references in the new app rather than forcing duplicate uploads.
• Risk that different project types require different gate configurations (scalability/configuration risk) — raised as a product design risk; impact: inability to apply one-size-fits-all governance; mitigation: design system to be extensible/configurable per project type so gates can be added per project-class.
• Risk that full automation is not feasible in the first release (scope/implementation risk) — raised as a development sequencing risk; impact: initial reliance on manual PM actions to mark gates and add evidence; mitigation: phased approach: prioritize Kiro governance→RDS sync first, then app, then Quicksight and later automation via Avoma/standardized meeting naming.
